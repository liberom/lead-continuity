## The Follow-Up Gap

Most businesses generate plenty of interest. Then it leaks. Old leads sit untouched in a CRM, inquiries wait too long for a response, missed calls never get recovered, and reviews get requested whenever someone remembers.

That's not a marketing strategy. It's an expensive way to lose revenue you already paid to create.

## The Numbers Don't Lie

- **27% of businesses** never follow up with a lead at all — [InsideSales/MIT LRM Study](https://www.leadresponsemanagement.org/lrm_study/)
- **The average response time** is 42 hours — but **78% of customers** buy from the company that responds first — [HBR, Oldroyd (2011)](https://hbr.org/2011/11/the-short-life-of-online-sales-leads)
- **80% of sales** require 5+ follow-ups, yet **44% of sales reps** give up after one — [National Sales Executive Association / HubSpot Research](https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/how-many-follow-up-calls-to-close-a-deal)
- **Missed calls** cost businesses an estimated $38,000 per year on average — [Ruby Receptionists Study](https://www.ruby.com/blog/missed-call-cost/)

## Why It Happens

It's not that businesses don't care. It's that the follow-up process is manual, inconsistent, and falls through the cracks when teams get busy. The phone rings during a job. A form submission comes in after hours. A past customer's information sits in a database that nobody has time to revisit.

These aren't strategic failures. They're operational ones.

## The Fix: Automated Continuity

The businesses that grow fastest don't have bigger teams or better ads. They have systems that ensure:

1. **Every lead gets an instant response** — within 60 seconds, not 42 hours
2. **Every missed call gets a text back** — before the caller dials your competitor
3. **Every past customer gets re-engaged** — on autopilot, not when someone remembers
4. **Every completed job triggers a review request** — systematically, not sporadically
